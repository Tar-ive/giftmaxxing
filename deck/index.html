The problem

A good gift is real work.

A present that actually lands takes time, attention, and genuinely knowing someone. Most people don't have all three — so gifting becomes a chore.

⏳

It takes time

Dozens of tabs, endless scrolling, and second-guessing — for one halfway-decent idea.

🧠

It takes insight

You have to read someone's taste — their vibe, not just the wishlist they never update.

🤝

It takes coordination

Group gifts mean spreadsheets, chasing Venmo, and one person fronting the cost.

The result: forgettable gifts — or a last-minute gift card.

Giftmaxxing Problem · 02
The insight

Gift-giving is a skill.

Some people just have it — they notice, they remember, they nail the thing you didn't know you wanted. Most of us don't, or can't spare the hours. So great gifting stays a privilege of the thoughtful and the time-rich.

What if it could be a commodity?
